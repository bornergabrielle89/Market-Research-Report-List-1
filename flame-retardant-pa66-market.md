<p><p>The Flame Retardant PA66 market comprises two primary types: halogen type and halogen-free type. Halogen-type flame retardants contain elements such as chlorine or bromine, providing effective fire resistance but raising environmental and health concerns. In contrast, halogen-free types utilize phosphorus, nitrogen, or inorganic materials, offering a safer alternative while still meeting fire safety standards. The choice between these types often reflects regulatory compliance, environmental considerations, and specific application requirements across automotive, electronics, and construction industries.</p></p>
