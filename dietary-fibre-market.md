<p><p>The dietary fiber market is primarily divided into two types: water-soluble and water-insoluble fibers. Water-soluble dietary fibers dissolve in water, forming a gel-like substance that aids in regulating blood sugar and cholesterol levels, often found in foods like oats and legumes. In contrast, water-insoluble dietary fibers do not dissolve in water, promoting bowel regularity and supporting digestive health; they are commonly found in whole grains, nuts, and vegetables. Both types contribute significantly to overall health and wellness.</p></p>
