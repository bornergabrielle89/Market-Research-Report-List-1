<p><p>The P-channel MOSFET market consists of two primary types: enhancement and depletion. Enhancement-type P-channel MOSFETs are designed to be off when no voltage is applied; they turn on with a negative gate-source voltage, allowing current to flow. In contrast, depletion-type P-channel MOSFETs are normally on; they require a positive gate-source voltage to turn off the current. Both types are crucial in power management, signal processing, and analog applications, influencing their demand in various electronics sectors.</p></p>
