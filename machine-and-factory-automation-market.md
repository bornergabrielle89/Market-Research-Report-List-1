<p><p>The machine and factory automation market is primarily divided into two types: continuous and intermittent automation. Continuous automation refers to processes that operate continually without interruption, ideal for high-volume production, such as in petrochemical or food processing industries. In contrast, intermittent automation involves equipment that operates in cycles, managing variable production runs and often utilized in manufacturing sectors like automotive or electronics, where flexibility and adaptability to changing demands are crucial. Both types enhance efficiency and consistency in operations.</p></p>
